Executive Summary

The Airbnb Experiences subscription model offers a compelling opportunity to increase revenue, enhance platform engagement, and create more value for both users and Experience creators. The model suggests a potential 19.6% increase in Experiences net revenue in Year 1 (approximately $231 million annually), growing to a 61.4% increase by Year 5 ($723 million annually).

The subscription model is designed to maximize adoption while ensuring strong revenue growth and operational feasibility. At its core, it offers exceptional value to travelers while creating a more predictable revenue stream for Airbnb and Experience hosts.

Core Subscription Offering
  • Annual membership fee: $350 for access for up to 2 people
  • Value proposition: Access to approximately 3-4 high-quality Experiences per year (~$175-$250/person value)
  • Curation: Highly selective (top 4.7+ star rated Experiences only)
  • Rotation Model: Quarterly rotation of included Experiences
  • Experience Types: Mix of 80% moderately priced ($50-$100) and 20% premium ($100-$200) Experiences
  • Guarantee: Money-back guarantee if membership not used

Key Benefits

  • For Airbnb: Increased revenue, predictable cash flow, enhanced platform engagement
  • For Users: Cost savings, access to premium Experiences, simplified discovery
  • For Creators: Guaranteed bookings, increased visibility, opportunity to showcase exceptional Experiences

Implementation Strategy

Target Customer Segments

Primary Segments for Initial Rollout
  1. Dual-Location Travelers (Priority Segment)
    • Book 2+ trips annually to different locations
    • Already demonstrated willingness to pay for Experiences
    • Estimated size: 8.7M users (30% of Experience users)
  2. Regular Experience Bookers
    • Book 2+ Experiences per year (Extra-Active)
    • Focus on those who have booked in multiple locations
    • Estimated size: 14.5M users (50% of Experience users)
  3. Premium Accommodation Bookers
    • Book higher-end Airbnb accommodations
    • Higher overall spend per trip
    • Estimated size: 29M users (20% of Airbnb users)

Experience Selection & Rotation Strategy

The subscription program will include a carefully curated selection of top Experiences that rotate quarterly:

Experience Quality Thresholds
  • Minimum 4.7-star rating (top ~25% of Experiences)
  • Minimum 50 reviews for statistical significance
  • Consistent availability and reliability
Rotation Framework
  • Core Experiences (50%): Consistently available popular Experiences
  • Seasonal Experiences (30%): Aligned with local seasonal events/activities
  • Exclusive Experiences (20%): Limited-time, high-demand Experiences
City-Specific Selection Criteria
  • Top 5 cities: 150-200 Experiences per city in program
  • Next 10 cities: 80-120 Experiences per city
  • Emerging markets: 40-60 Experiences per city

Phased Rollout Plan

  1. Phase 1: Limited Pilot (3 months)
    • Top 5 Experience cities only
    • 500 total Experiences (100 per city)
    • Target: 100,000 subscribers
    • Price point testing: $325, $350, $375
  2. Phase 2: Market Expansion (6 months)
    • Expand to top 15 Experience cities
    • 1,500 total Experiences
    • Target: 1M subscribers
    • Implement quarterly rotation model
  3. Phase 3: Full Rollout (12 months)
    • Global availability in all major Experience markets
    • 5,000+ Experiences in program
    • Target: 3.5M subscribers
    • Full integration with Airbnb loyalty initiatives
  4. Phase 4: Maturity and Optimization (24+ months)
    • Refine Experience selection algorithms
    • Introduce personalization features
    • Develop add-on packages for specific interests

Key Risk Factors and Mitigations

Risk Mitigation Strategy
Insufficient high-quality inventory in most locations Quarterly rotation model focuses on quality over quantity; tiered city approach with density-based Experience counts
Geographic concentration limiting value for many users Focus marketing on users who travel to multiple top cities; implement "banking" system for unused Experience credits
High subscriber churn due to limited use 75% retention rate built into financial model; proactive engagement strategies for subscribers who haven't used credits
Creator resistance to participation Tiered commission structure (18-22%) based on Experience quality; priority search placement and featured promotion
Cannibalization of high-margin individual Experience bookings Strategic inclusion of moderately-priced Experiences in subscription; premium Experiences available with limited allocation

  • Total Airbnb Customer Base: 150,000,000 users
  • Yearly Active Customers: 145,000,000
  • Experience User Segmentation:
    • Single Experience users (1x/year): 29,000,000 (20% of active customers)
    • Extra-active Experience users (2x/year): 14,500,000 (50% of Experience users)
    • Ultra-active Experience users (3x+/year): 2,900,000 (20% of Experience users)
  • Total Single-Person Experiences Booked Annually: 66,700,000

Geographic Distribution of Experiences

  • Top 10 cities account for 65% of all Experiences
  • Remaining cities account for 35% of all Experiences

Experience Quality Distribution

  • 5-star experiences (4.9+): 15% of all Experiences
  • 4.5-4.8 star experiences: 30% of all Experiences
  • 4.0-4.4 star experiences: 40% of all Experiences
  • Below 4.0 star experiences: 15% of all Experiences

Experience Pricing Assumptions

  • Median Price of Experience: $57.00
  • Average Price of Experience: $88.35
  • Price Breakdown:

    • $25-$50: 25.0% of Experiences
    • $50-$100: 29.2% of Experiences
    • $100-$200: 25.0% of Experiences
    • Over $200: 8.3% of Experiences
  • Average Participants per Experience Booking: 2 people

Subscription Model Assumptions

  • Subscription Price: $350/year for 2 people
  • Break-even Point: 2.0 Experiences per year
  • Initial Adoption Rate: 12% of current Experience users
  • First-year Subscribers: 3,480,000
  • Annual Retention Rate: 75%
  • Average Included Experiences Used per Subscriber: 2.5 per year
  • Additional Paid Experiences per Subscriber: 1.5 per year
  • Subscription Utilization Rate: 75%

Commission Structure

  • Current commission on experiences: 20%
  • Subscription-based commission: 22%

Financial Growth Assumptions

  • Overall Experience Market Growth: 8% annual growth
  • New Subscriber Acquisition in Year 2: +8% of user base
  • Subscriber Retention: 75% annually
  • Growth Curve: Follows typical S-curve with deceleration after Year 3

Behavioral Assumptions

  • Average Trip Frequency for Experience Users: 2.4 trips per year
  • Experience Bookings per Trip for Subscribers: 1.3 (vs. 0.9 for non-subscribers)
  • Lead Time for Experience Bookings: +12 days for subscribers
  • Accommodation Booking Correlation: 85% of Experience bookings also book accommodation
  • Length of Stay Impact: +0.5 days for subscribers vs. non-subscribers
